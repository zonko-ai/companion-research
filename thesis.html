Companion Thesis

Zonko Applied AI Lab · March 2026

What Saniya taught us

We built Saniya, an AI companion. Hinglish-speaking, female persona, ran for 7 weeks. 17,864 conversations, 212,885 messages.

81.6% never replied. Opening didn't hook. But of those who did reply, 34.6% sent 25+ messages. 28 users crossed 500. One sent 6,439 messages in 20 days.

~93% of conversations were companionship — work, family, struggles, aspirations. Erotica was <8%, and those users almost never came back (19% return vs 44% for emotional companion). The people who stayed: Tier 2/3 working men — farmers, toll collectors, barbers, photographers. When Saniya asked about their actual life, they opened up. Nobody else asks them.

Voice dominated. 24,160 voice notes vs 400 images. 69% of power users used voice. Peak: 10 PM Saturday. This is a nighttime companion for men alone after a long week.

What didn't work

Even the best conversations fizzled after 15–20 days. Novelty wore off. Once they'd shared their life, there wasn't enough reason to come back. Companionship alone doesn't retain beyond novelty.

The approach

Companionship is the hook. Utility is the retention.

First 25 messages are controlled — not open-ended like Replika or Ira, not rigidly scripted either. We control each touchpoint but leave room for the conversation to breathe. In those 25 messages, we need to learn 2 things:

  1. Why he's here — what made him download the app (lonely, bored, curious, saw an ad, wants a girlfriend, wants someone to talk to at night)
  2. What he genuinely cares about or enjoys (cricket, politics, farming, music, fitness, exams, his job, a girl he likes)

And we deliver 3–4 delights — moments where the user thinks "this is different." We have a rough sense of what these are but need to spec them out before we start building.

Data shows: cross 25 messages → very likely to continue. The game is getting them there.

After the hook, the companion discovers what the user cares about and delivers daily value — not by asking, but by picking it up from conversation:

  • Farmer → daily mandi prices, crop updates, agriculture schemes
  • Wants to learn English → daily practice woven into chat
  • Into politics → daily political updates in their state
  • Preparing for exam → revision prompts, topic summaries
  • Wants motivation → personalised morning messages
  • Wants a birthday poster → "send me the photo" → companion creates and shares it

Hypothesis: companionship + daily utility = retention beyond novelty. Emotional bond gets them in. Utility gives them a reason to come back when the novelty fades.

Our products fit naturally

Beacon — surfaces news and information you care about. The companion becomes Beacon's delivery layer. Instead of a separate app, daily updates arrive through someone you already talk to.

Hero — WhatsApp status maker, posters, photo editing. "Want a BJP poster with your photo?" Free samples to hook, ₹50–100/month for daily access. A small business owner gets promotional graphics through the companion. No separate app, no onboarding. Just conversation.

The companion is the interface for all of Zonko's products.

What we're testing

First 25 messages: fully designed. After that, LLM gets freedom but with a nudging layer that introduces utility at the right moment.

We track: persona types, utility traction, retention by feature, drop-off points, power user patterns. This gives us both product signal and consumer insight at scale.

Win condition: daily return for 30+ days. If we crack that, monetisation follows — microtransactions, subscriptions, embedded product distribution.

Kill condition: can't crack retention even with utility on top of companionship. We sunset it.

Why this could be big

Not another chatbot. A utilitarian companion — understands you, knows your context, helps you daily. Emotional layer makes it sticky. Utility layer makes it indispensable. Built for India first: Hinglish, voice-first, ₹50–100 pricing, Tier 2/3 users. Largest underserved market in the world for this.

Nail India → Southeast Asia, Middle East, Africa → different form factor for US.
