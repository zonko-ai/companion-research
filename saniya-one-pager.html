Early users (Feb 9 cohort) were highest quality -- 31.4% activation, 18.6% depth. As ads scaled to lower-quality audiences, both metrics collapsed. The Feb 9 cohort is your real signal for what the product can do with the right audience.

The Five Things That Matter

1. Fix the first message or nothing else matters

82.7% silence rate means the opening is broken. Every other optimization is downstream of this. Test: voice note opener, specific cultural question, suggested reply buttons. The Feb 9 cohort's 31.4% activation proves it CAN work with the right audience + right opening.

2. The 428 multi-day users are your North Star

Not the 1,207 deep users. Not the 2,814 activated. The 428 who came back on a different day. These are the ones who formed a bond, not a binge. Study them. What topic buckets are they in? What intent tags dominate? What did Saniya say that made them return? This is your PMF signal.

3. Media users are a different species

8.4x more likely to go deep. 37.7% multi-day vs 3.7%. These users treat Saniya like a real person -- they send voice notes, photos. The product should INVITE media interaction after the first reply, not wait for users to discover it. Voice-first onboarding may solve both activation AND retention.

4. Decide: emotional companion or entertainment

The Erotica vs Emotional Companion split is the fundamental product question. Erotica drives session depth (binge). Emotional Companion drives multi-day return (bond). You can't optimize for both. Chai chose engagement metrics and hit $70M ARR. Replika chose emotional support and hit $24M. Different products, different outcomes, different users.

5. The Positive Interaction tag is your wow moment tracker

Cross-reference Positive Interaction signals with multi-day users to find the specific conversation patterns that create the "she gets me" moment. This is where the product magic lives. Memory callbacks, emotional mirroring, gentle challenges, inside jokes -- which of these actually appear in conversations that lead to return visits?
