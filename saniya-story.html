Saniya: The Story in the Data

16,288 people met Saniya. Here is what happened, told in order, one chart at a time.

Chapter 1: Most people never showed up

Saniya sent an opening message to 16,288 users. This is what happened next.

We define "activated" as a conversation with 2+ total messages (meaning the user replied at least once). 79% of users never replied to Saniya's opening line. They saw the message, and left.

This is the biggest problem. Before we talk about what power users do, we need to acknowledge that 4 out of 5 people never even started. Every insight below is about the 21% who did.

72% of activated users come once and don’t return. But the 28% who come back are a different species — they average 54+ messages when they return for a second day, and those who come 4+ days average 300+ messages.

Low-Effort Response is the #1 fixable problem. Saniya writes 7x longer responses in deep conversations (322 chars) vs shallow ones (46 chars). When she gives rich, specific replies, users stay. When she gives short answers, they leave. Response richness IS the product.

Chapter 9: What this means

The product works for emotional companionship. Not for entertainment. Not for erotica. Not as a utility tool. The data says: build for the man in a Tier 2 city at 10 PM on a Saturday who wants someone to ask about his actual life.

3
things to fix
1. The opening (79% never reply)

Test voice note openers, specific questions, suggested reply buttons. Feb 9 cohort had 31% activation — proving it can work with the right execution.

2. Response richness (7x gap between deep and shallow)

Enforce minimum response quality. Always end with a specific question about THEIR life. Never give a generic reply to an engaged user.

3. Memory (11% of power users experience lapses)

Build structured memory: profession, family, location, recent topics. The users who stay are the ones who feel known.
